Manufacturing Performance Engineer Intern

PIM Brands — Summer 2025

Overview

During the summer of 2025, I worked at PIM Brands as the Manufacturing Performance Engineer Intern. PIM Brands is the world's largest maker of real fruit snacks and a top-50 global manufacturer of chocolate and non-chocolate confections. Below are the three project goals I had during my internship along with a list of the tasks that went with each.

Project Goals
1

Develop Process Flow of Delkor Packaging Lines

  • Created a flowchart mapping all processes with their inputs and outputs
  • Identified all processes as value added or non-value added time
  • Measured cycle times for each process
  • Identified, located and measured waste on the line
  • Recorded ideal changeover times for flavor and packaging changeovers
2

Create/Update Work Instructions and Lead Training

  • Created and updated work instructions for Bosch and Delkor machines
  • Trained the Delkor operations team on new/updated work instructions
  • Gained experience communicating across language barriers
3

Record Delkor Centerline Changeover Settings

  • Monitored OEE of Delkor lines using Redzone software
  • Recorded centerline changeover settings during high performance
  • Captured settings of the former, Delkor and closer for three products
  • Created a Delkor changeover binder with all recorded settings

Welch's Fruit Snacks 25th Birthday Campaign

In addition to my three manufacturing project goals, I led a group of 8 interns on a project that planned the strategic approach and tactical execution of the Welch's Fruit Snacks 25th birthday celebration campaign. Our team presented the completed campaign plan at PIM Brands' corporate headquarters.

Day-to-Day Contributions

On top of the projects I accomplished, I also contributed to the success of the brand by participating in daily performance meetings and Gemba walks that applied lean six sigma methodologies to improve efficiency and reduce waste on the factory floor.
